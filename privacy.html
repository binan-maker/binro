Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2024

1. Introduction

QR Guard ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

By using QR Guard, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

We do NOT collect:

  • Personal identification information (name, email, phone number)
  • Payment information or financial data
  • Location data
  • Contacts or address book information
  • Photos or media files

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

Device Information (Anonymous):

  • Device model and Android version
  • App version and usage statistics
  • Crash reports and performance data

QR Code Data (Anonymous & Aggregated):

  • QR code content hash (not the actual content)
  • Scan timestamp
  • Community safety reports (if you choose to report)

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide and maintain the QR Guard app
  • Enable community safety features (scam reports, trust scores)
  • Improve app performance and user experience
  • Fix bugs and technical issues
  • Comply with legal obligations

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Local Storage

All your scan history is stored ONLY on your device. We do not upload your personal scan history to our servers.

4.2 Cloud Storage (Anonymous Data Only)

Anonymous, aggregated data is stored on Firebase (Google Cloud Platform) servers located in India (asia-south1 region) to comply with India's DPDP Act 2023.

4.3 Security Measures

  • Data encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS)
  • No personal data stored on servers
  • Regular security audits
  • Compliance with DPDP Act 2023

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do NOT sell, trade, or rent your personal information.

We may share anonymous, aggregated data in the following situations:

  • Service Providers: With Firebase (Google) for app functionality and analytics
  • Legal Requirements: If required by law or to protect our rights
  • Community Features: Anonymous QR code safety reports are shared with other users (without any personal identifiers)

6. Your Rights

Under the DPDP Act 2023, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request information about what data we process
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your data (uninstall the app)
  • Data Portability: Export your data (scan history stored on your device)
  • Grievance Redressal: Contact us at privacy@qrguard.app

7. Children's Privacy

QR Guard is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by:

  • Posting the new Privacy Policy on this page
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Prominent in-app notification (for significant changes)

9. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us:

  • Email: privacy@qrguard.app
  • Website: https://qrguard.github.io
  • Data Protection Officer: dpo@qrguard.app

10. Compliance

QR Guard is compliant with:

  • Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 (India)
  • Google Play Developer Program Policies
  • Kerala Startup Mission (KSUM) Guidelines
