Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 8, 2026

1. Who We Are

BinRo is a beta-stage mobile application designed to help users scan, analyse, generate, and manage QR codes. We are a private software project operating under the laws of India. This policy applies to all users of BinRo on Android, iOS, and web platforms.

BinRo is in early access, so we're refining features and policies as we grow. If we ever make a meaningful change, we'll simply ask you to review and re-accept it.

2. Data We Collect

By using BinRo, you agree we collect the following categories of information:

2.1 Account & Identity Data

  • Email address, display name, profile photo (if uploaded)
  • Google account ID and authentication tokens (if using Google Sign-In)
  • Password hash (never stored in plaintext) for email accounts

2.2 QR Code & Scan Data

  • The content of QR codes you scan — such as links, contact info, Wi-Fi details, plain text, or payment info like a UPI ID and merchant name — so we can check it's safe
  • Timestamp, device platform, and app version at time of each scan
  • QR codes you generate, their destinations, and scan counts
  • Community reports, votes, and comments you submit on any QR code

2.3 Usage & Behavioural Data

  • In-app navigation patterns, feature usage frequency, and session duration
  • Scan velocity data (how often a particular QR code is scanned across all users)
  • Search queries entered within the app
  • Friend connections, follows, and social interactions

2.4 Device & Network Data

  • Device type, OS version, screen resolution, and app version
  • IP address and approximate geographic location derived from IP
  • Network type (Wi-Fi or mobile data)
  • Crash logs and error reports for debugging

2.5 Camera & Media Data

  • Camera frames are processed locally on your device for QR detection. Images are NOT uploaded to our servers unless you explicitly upload a gallery image for scanning.

3. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used for the following purposes. By using BinRo, you consent to all of these uses:

3.1 Core Service Delivery

  • Authenticate your account, maintain sessions, and secure your profile
  • Perform QR code safety analysis using heuristics, blacklists, and community data
  • Sync scan history, favourites, and generated QR codes across your devices
  • Power community trust scores from aggregated, weighted user reports
  • Send in-app notifications about QR codes you follow or report activity

3.2 Learning & Getting Smarter

  • Aggregated, anonymised scan data and safety reports help train and improve our threat detection models — so BinRo gets better at spotting scams for everyone.
  • URL and domain patterns from scanned QR codes contribute to our pattern-recognition database, helping us catch new threats early.
  • Community report patterns (which codes users flag as safe/scam/fake/spam) help improve how accurately we classify risk.
  • Your usage patterns are analysed to improve the app's experience and guide what we build next.
  • We may use anonymised scan data to build threat intelligence datasets. Your personal identity is never included.

3.3 Advertising & Keeping BinRo Free

  • Aggregated, non-personally-identifiable usage patterns may be used to show relevant in-app ads, which help keep BinRo free.
  • Device type, approximate region, and general usage category may be shared with advertising partners to serve relevant ads.
  • We never share your name, email address, specific QR scan content, or payment data with advertisers.
  • You can opt out of personalised advertising anytime by contacting ahmedsameerbinan2@gmail.com. This won't remove all ads, just the personalised ones.

3.4 Security, Legal & Compliance

  • Detect and prevent abuse, fraud, spam, and coordinated trust score manipulation
  • Comply with applicable Indian law, government orders, and court directives
  • Respond to law enforcement requests as required by applicable law

4. Data Pattern Analysis & Database Use

BinRo maintains a continuously updated threat intelligence database derived from community scan data and reports. This database powers our real-time QR code safety analysis.

  • Scan patterns across our entire user base are analysed to detect emerging fraud campaigns, phishing waves, and new malicious QR code formats.
  • Payment QR codes (UPI, BharatQR, etc.) are analysed in aggregate for unusual merchant patterns that may indicate fraud.
  • URL and domain patterns from scanned QR codes are added to our threat pattern database and may be shared with our threat intelligence partners in anonymised form.
  • Community report data is used to build statistical models that weight reporter credibility and detect coordinated abuse.

All pattern analysis databases use anonymised and aggregated data. We do not build databases that link specific QR code content to individual users.

5. Firebase & Third-Party Infrastructure

BinRo uses Google Firebase for backend infrastructure. Your data is stored in Firebase Firestore, Firebase Realtime Database, and Firebase Auth. Firebase is compliant with GDPR, SOC 2, and ISO 27001.

We also use: Google Safe Browsing API (URL threat checking), Razorpay (web donation processing), and third-party analytics and crash reporting tools. These services have their own privacy policies which govern their use of data they receive.

We are not responsible for the data practices of these third-party providers. Links to their privacy policies are available on request.

6. Data Retention

  • Account data is retained while your account is active.
  • Upon account deletion, personally identifiable data is removed from active databases within 30 days.
  • Anonymised, aggregated data (scan counts, threat patterns, trust scores) may be retained indefinitely as part of our threat intelligence database.
  • Backup copies may be retained for up to 90 days post-deletion for disaster recovery purposes.
  • Data submitted as community reports may persist in anonymised form even after account deletion.

7. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We may share data in the following circumstances:

  • With Google Firebase as our primary infrastructure and authentication provider.
  • With advertising partners: aggregated, non-personally-identifiable usage data only.
  • With AI/ML partners: anonymised scan patterns and threat data for model improvement.
  • With threat intelligence sharing networks: anonymised URL/domain/QR threat patterns.
  • When required by law, court order, government directive, or to protect public safety.
  • In the event of acquisition or merger, under equivalent privacy protections.

We never sell your name, email address, phone number, specific QR scan content, payment data, or any directly personally identifiable information to third parties for commercial purposes.

8. Government & Law Enforcement Requests

BinRo operates under Indian law. We comply with lawful government and law enforcement requests for user data, including those made under the Information Technology Act, 2000, the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and applicable court orders.

Where legally permitted to do so, we will notify affected users of such requests. We review all government data requests for legal validity before complying.

We do not provide government agencies with backdoor access to our systems or databases. All data access requires valid legal process.

9. Data Security & Breach Policy

All data in transit between your device and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2+. Firebase Auth uses industry-standard OAuth 2.0 and secure token management. Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256 where Firebase infrastructure supports it.

We take security seriously and continuously monitor for issues. If something ever did affect your data, here's exactly what you can expect from us: we'll notify you by email or in-app message as soon as possible, inform relevant authorities as required by law, and act quickly to contain and resolve the issue.

10. Profile Privacy Controls

Private Account
