5. QR Code & UPI / Payment QR Data

BinRo specialises in scanning and analysing payment QR codes, including UPI QR codes, BharatQR, and QR codes from 80+ Indian payment apps. Here is how we handle this sensitive data:

5.1 What UPI / Payment QR Codes Contain

A typical UPI QR code encodes publicly visible merchant information such as:

  • Merchant / payee Virtual Payment Address (VPA), e.g., merchant@upi
  • Merchant name or business name
  • Optional: requested amount and transaction note
  • Optional: BharatQR merchant category code (MCC)

This information is publicly embedded in the QR code and is required for the payment to work. BinRo analyses this data to detect fraudulent or cloned merchant QR codes.

5.2 How We Process Payment QR Data

  • The decoded content of payment QR codes you scan is transmitted to our servers over an encrypted TLS connection for safety analysis.
  • We check the merchant VPA and associated metadata against our fraud detection database, community reports, and threat intelligence feeds.
  • We do not initiate, authorise, or complete any UPI payment on your behalf. BinRo is a scanning and analysis tool only — it does not integrate with UPI payment rails or execute fund transfers.
  • Scanned payment QR content is stored in your scan history, linked to your account, for your personal reference.
  • Merchant VPAs and patterns from scanned QR codes are added to our anonymised threat intelligence database in aggregate. Your identity is not linked to this database.

5.3 BharatQR & Multi-App Support

BinRo parses QR code formats across 80+ Indian payment applications including PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, BHIM, Amazon Pay, and others. This parsing is performed on-device. The decoded content may be sent to our servers for safety analysis as described above.

ℹ️

BinRo is not a payment app. We do not hold, transfer, or process funds. We are a security verification tool. If you have payment-related issues, contact your payment provider directly.

7. Data Pattern Analysis & Threat Intelligence

BinRo maintains a continuously updated threat intelligence database derived from community scan data and reports. This database is the engine that powers our real-time QR code safety scores.

  • Scan pattern analysis: Aggregate scan patterns across all users are analysed to detect emerging fraud campaigns, phishing waves, and new malicious QR code formats. For example, if thousands of users scan the same fraudulent QR code in a short window, our system flags it as high-risk.
  • Payment QR fraud detection: Payment QR codes (UPI, BharatQR) are analysed in aggregate for unusual merchant patterns, VPA registration anomalies, and known fraud signatures.
  • URL & domain database: URLs and domains decoded from scanned QR codes are added to our threat pattern database in anonymised form and may be shared with threat intelligence partners.
  • Community report modelling: Report patterns are used to build statistical models that weight reporter credibility and detect coordinated abuse (such as attempts to falsely flag legitimate merchants).
  • AI model training: Anonymised scan content and safety labels may be used to train and improve our machine learning threat detection models. Your personal identity is never included in any training dataset.
✅

All pattern analysis uses anonymised and aggregated data only. We do not build databases that link specific QR code content to identifiable individuals.

10. Data Security & Breach Policy

10.1 Security Measures

  • In transit: All data between your device and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher. We enforce HTTPS-only connections.
  • At rest: Data stored in Firebase Firestore and Firebase Storage is encrypted at rest using AES-256 by Google Cloud infrastructure.
  • Authentication: Firebase Auth uses industry-standard OAuth 2.0, secure token management, and refresh token rotation.
  • Passwords: Email account passwords are hashed using bcrypt with a unique salt. Plain-text passwords are never stored or logged anywhere in our systems.
  • API security: Our backend APIs use authentication token validation, rate limiting, input sanitisation, and security headers on all endpoints.
  • Access control: Internal access to production data is restricted on a strict need-to-know basis with audit logging.

10.2 Data Breach Response

Despite our best efforts, no system is completely immune to security incidents. If a breach occurs that affects your personal data, here is what you can expect from us:

  • Notification: We will notify you by email and/or in-app message as soon as practicably possible — and within 72 hours of discovering the breach, in line with applicable data protection law.
  • Regulatory reporting: We will report the breach to relevant authorities as required by the DPDPA 2023 and any other applicable law.
  • Containment: We will immediately take steps to contain the breach, revoke compromised credentials, and secure affected systems.
  • Transparency: We will provide a clear account of what data was affected, what steps we are taking, and what you should do to protect yourself.
